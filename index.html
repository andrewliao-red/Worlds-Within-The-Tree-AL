About The Journey

Worlds Within the Tree is a digital, exploratory archive that invites users to navigate and engage with a collection of pieces through familiar, game-based interaction.

Rather than presenting archival material in a static or purely-informational format, the project reimagines the archive as an interactive, living, and navigable environment—one that encourages movement, discovery, and personal interpretation.

Structured as a series of interconnected “realms,” each space functions as both a container and a lens, framing different forms of media, ideas, and narratives. The yggdrasil tree serves as a central metaphor: a growing, branching system of memory, knowledge, and experience. Just as a tree expands outward through its branches, the archive extends through these worlds, allowing users to move between them in a non-linear and exploratory way.

By combining principles of game design with archival thinking, Worlds Within the Tree challenges traditional expectations of how collections are accessed and understood. It shifts the role of the user from a passive viewer to an active participant, where interaction becomes a method of reading, and navigation becomes a form of interpretation.
